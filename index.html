Coronary heart disease (CHD):
is a condition that occurs when the coronary arteries,
which supply blood to the heart muscle, become narrowed or blocked due to a buildup of plaque (a mixture of fat, cholesterol, and other substances). This narrowing can reduce blood flow to the heart, leading to various problems
including:
Angina:
Pain or discomfort that occurs when the heart doesn't get enough blood.
Heart Attack:
A complete blockage of blood flow to a part of the heart muscle, causing damage or death to that tissue.
Arrhythmias:
Irregular heartbeats that can result from disrupted blood flow.
Heart Failure:
A condition where the heart cannot pump effectively, often resulting from chronic CHD.
Risk factors for CHD:
Include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, lack of physical activity, and a family history of heart disease. Preventative measures focus on lifestyle changes, medication, and sometimes surgical interventions to restore blood flow.

Features of AI Model

Collection: Gather a diverse dataset of medical imaging (e.g., CT, MRI, angiograms) and patient health records that include historical data on plaque formation.

CNN Implementation: Use Convolutional Neural Networks to analyze and extract spatial features from medical images, focusing on identifying characteristics of arterial plaque.

RNN Integration: Implement Recurrent Neural Networks to analyze time-series data, capturing changes in patient conditions and risk factors over time.

Feature Engineering: Combine features from both the CNN and RNN outputs to create a comprehensive representation of plaque status and progression.

Model Training: Train the model using a robust dataset, ensuring it can generalize well across different populations and imaging modalities.

Validation and Testing: Employ cross-validation techniques to assess model performance and minimize overfitting, ensuring reliable predictions.
